''[[Doomsday Clock (comics)|Doomsday Clock]]'' is a [[Superhero comics|superhero]] [[comic book]] [[Limited series (comics)|limited series]] published by [[DC Comics]], created by [[Geoff Johns]], [[Gary Frank (comics)|Gary Frank]] and Brad Anderson.<ref name="ign_announce" /> As a direct sequel to the [[graphic novel]] ''[[Watchmen]]'' by [[Alan Moore]], [[Dave Gibbons]] and [[John Higgins (comics)|John Higgins]],<ref name="screenrant_sequel" /> this series concluded the plot established between [[The New 52]] and [[DC Rebirth]], featuring a massive roster of characters owned by DC Comics.<ref name="screenrant_sequel" />

The following is a list of characters who have appeared.

==''Watchmen'' universe==
===Crimebusters===
The '''Crimebusters''' is a superhero group that succeeds the Minutemen. The group was short-lived when the Keene Act that forbade non-government sanctioned superheroes was passed.

Among its notable members are:

* '''[[Comedian (character)|Comedian / Edward Blake]]''' — A former member of the Minutemen and the Crimebusters whose death was prevented by Doctor Manhattan.{{efn-ur|name=#2}} He then began his revenge crusade against Ozymandias for attempting to kill him, as well as continuing on the trail of Mime and Marionette.{{efn-ur|name=#5}} Comedian catches up to Joker, Mime, and Marionette when they attend an underground villain meeting held by Riddler to discuss the Superman Theory. He makes himself known by shooting Typhoon in the face. While pursuing them, he evades Giganta's attacks, shoots Riddler in the leg, and uses a grenade to defeat the other villains present. When Mime and Marionette are in bed together the next morning, Comedian catches up to them where he plans to use them to find Ozymandias. Before Comedian can do anything, Joker comes to Mime and Marionette's rescue and stuns Comedian with a joy buzzer.{{efn-ur|name=#6}} As Batman gets free and fights Joker, Mime and Marionette escape with Comedian and the Lantern Battery.{{efn-ur|name=#7}} After non-fatally shooting Oyzmandias, Comedian is returned to his spot in time.{{efn-ur|name=#12}}
* '''[[Doctor Manhattan|Doctor Manhattan / Jonathan Osterman]]''' — A former member of the Crimebusters who remains skeptical of humanity's hope. He is suspected for altering the DC Universe following the [[Flashpoint (comics)|Flashpoint]] event, and for making "experiments" in the lives of Superman and everyone else. When he was summoned by Ozymandias through Bubastis II, Manhattan reveals about Ozymandias's false cancer, and why he spared the Mime and Marionette's firstborn child.{{efn-ur|name=#7}} After Firestorm's incident, Manhattan was confronted by most of Earth's heroes, but he defeated them all.{{efn-ur|name=#8}}{{efn-ur|name=#9}} Before arriving on Earth to confront Superman, Manhattan spares the defeated heroes on Mars, but makes sure they don't interfere.{{efn-ur|name=#10}} After Superman stops Pozhar from attacking him, Doctor Manhattan has a chat with Superman before undoing the effects that erased the Justice Society of America and the Legion of Super-Heroes.{{efn-ur|name=#12}}
* '''[[Nite Owl#Dan Dreiberg|Nite Owl II / Daniel Dreiberg]]''' — Former member of the Crimebusters, who has a current relationship with Laurel Juspeczyk. They would conceive a daughter and adopt Mime and Marionette's son.{{efn-ur|name=#11}} Dan and Laurel are confirmed to have adopted Mime and Marionette's son who addressed himself as Clark.{{efn-ur|name=#12}}
* '''[[Ozymandias (Watchmen)|Ozymandias / Adrian Veidt]]''' — A former member of the Crimebusters who becomes a fugitive after the truth behind his alien monster's attack is exposed when Walter's journal was published. Having purportedly been diagnosed with terminal brain cancer, and knowing that his plan to save the world has failed, Veidt recruits Reggie Long as the new Rorschach and has him break Erika Manson and her husband Marcos Maez out of prison. When Rorschach II returns with the two, Veidt reveals himself and his situation, explaining to the two criminals that they must follow Manhattan to another universe and convince him to save their world.{{efn-ur|name=#1}} Using the Owlship, Veidt and his group travel to the DC Universe just as nuclear war breaks out on their Earth. After conducting research on this new world he's found himself in, Veidt goes to Metropolis to ask Lex Luthor to join his quest. However, as he is pleading his case, Veidt is shocked to find himself being confronted by the Comedian, who has been transported to the DC Universe by Manhattan.{{efn-ur|name=#2}} Comedian turns out to be evenly matched with Veidt, forcing him to retreat through Luthor's office window. Veidt falls twenty stories and is hospitalized with minor injuries, but soon manages to escape.{{efn-ur|name=#3}}{{efn-ur|name=#5}} Upon returning to the Owlship, Veidt is confronted by Batman, who has read the contents of the original Rorschach's journal. As the two elude the police, Batman asserts that Veidt murdered millions as part of a delusional [[hero syndrome]], and accuses him of concocting a conspiracy theory that has negatively affected the public's trust in the superheroes of the DC Universe. Veidt in turn criticizes Batman for focusing all his attention on chasing supervillains while ignoring the world's social problems. A struggle ensues, leading to Batman falling out of the Owlship and into a mob of anti-hero protestors below.{{efn-ur|name=#5}} Using his pet lynx, Bubastis II, and Alan Scott's Green Lantern Power Battery, Veidt teleports Doctor Manhattan to the Joker's lair, where Batman is fighting Marionette and Mime. Manhattan refuses to return to their world since he's in the middle of experimenting with this one. Manhattan reveals, among other things, that Veidt lied to Rorschach II about having cancer in order to get his help. Rorschach II punches Veidt and flees, while Veidt returns to the Owlship, attacks Imra and Johnny, and declares he can save everyone.{{efn-ur|name=#7}} He breaks into the Oval Office at the [[White House]] and checks the file of Firestorm. He then watches the event involving Superman and Firestorm in Russia from an undisclosed location with what appears to be a military outfit lying behind him,{{efn-ur|name=#8}} with Imra and Johnny being held captive.{{efn-ur|name=#11}} After claiming that he arranged for Superman to convince Doctor Manhattan to save the world, Ozymandias is non-fatally shot by Comedian. When returned to his world, Ozymandias is last seen standing trial.{{efn-ur|name=#12}}
* '''[[Rorschach (character)|Rorschach I / Walter Kovacs]]''' — Former member of the Crimebusters, who was killed by Doctor Manhattan seven years ago. However, his journal was published over time by [[Robert Redford]] and the ''New Frontiersman'', exposing Ozymandias' role in the New York massacre.{{efn-ur|name=#1}} According to Ozymandias, Walter had broken down Malcolm Long's positive attitude during his time in jail.{{efn-ur|name=#7}}
* '''[[Silk Spectre#Laurie Juspeczyk|Silk Spectre II / Laurel Juspeczyk]]''' — Former member of the Crimebusters and daughter of Edward Blake and Sally Jupiter. She would conceive a daughter with Nite Owl II and adopt Mime and Marionette's son.{{efn-ur|name=#11}} She refused to join Ozymandias on his search to find Doctor Manhattan.{{efn-ur|name=#7}}

===Minutemen===
The '''Minutemen''' are a superhero group that came before the Crimebusters. The group was founded in 1939 during the Golden Age. The group later disbanded in 1949 following some public controversies.

Among its notable members are:

* '''[[List of Watchmen characters#Captain Metropolis|Captain Metropolis / Nelson Gardner]]''' — Founding member of the Minutemen who would later found the Crimebusters. He was found decapitated in a road accident.
* '''[[List of Watchmen characters#Dollar Bill|Dollar Bill / William Brady]]''' — Former member of the Minutemen who died trying to stop a bank robbery just because his cape that the bank he works for made for him got caught in the revolving door.
* '''[[List of Watchmen characters#Hooded Justice|Hooded Justice]]''' - Former member of the Minutemen who had a relationship with Captain Metropolis. He was murdered by Nite Owl I after the Comedian framed him for a series of child killings caused by serial killer Rolf Müller.
* '''[[List of Watchmen characters#Mothman|Mothman / Byron Lewis]]''' — Former member of the Minutemen. Rorschach II met him in an asylum following the attack of Ozymandias' alien monster, and learned some fighting moves from him. Byron later commits suicide by being attracted to the fires of the burning asylum.{{efn-ur|name=#4}} He used to hide the pages in Dr. Long's journal that show his mental breakdown during his time with Rorschach I.{{efn-ur|name=#7}}
* '''[[Nite Owl#Hollis Mason|Nite Owl I / Hollis Mason]]''' — Former member of the Minutemen who died fighting the thugs that attacked his home.
* '''[[List of Watchmen characters#Silhouette|Silhouette / Ursula Zand]]''' — Former member of the Minutemen who was killed by the Liquidator.
* '''[[Silk Spectre#Sally Jupiter|Silk Spectre I / Sally Jupiter]]''' — Former member of the Minutemen, who still loved Edward Blake in spite of his past cruelties, giving birth to their daughter Laurel. She was mentioned to have passed away.{{efn-ur|name=#4}}

===New Frontiersman===
The '''New Frontiersman''' is a newspaper that published Rorschach's journal, exposing Ozymandias' role in the New York Massacre.

* '''Seymour David''' — A junior worker at the ''New Frontiersman'' magazine offices. It was revealed that he was “brutally beaten to death” after the publication of Walter's journal.{{efn-ur|name=#1}}

===Ozymandias' team===
* '''Bubastis I''' — A genetically-altered [[lynx]] that was the former pet of Ozymandias.{{efn-ur|name=#4}}
* '''Bubastis II''' — A genetically-altered lynx that is the current pet of Ozymandias and the successor of the original Bubastis. It was later revealed to have been cloned from the original Bubastis as it had some of the energy needed to track Doctor Manhattan.{{efn-ur|name=#7}} She later uses that same energy to cause an explosion on Russia around Firestorm and Superman to frame Manhattan.{{efn-ur|name=#8}}{{efn-ur|name=#11}}
* '''Mime / Marcos Maez and Marionette / Erika Manson''' — A criminal husband-and-wife team. Based on the ''[[Casey and Andy]]'' character of the same name by [[Andy Weir]], Mime is a [[mime artist]]-themed villain who wields invisible weapons while Marionette wields wires. Erika grew up around her father's marionette shop, where she befriended the mute Marcos. When her father hung himself after being threatened by crooked cops, she and Marcos badly beat up the cops and pursued a life of crime. She was sent to the [[orphanage]] in [[Grand Forks, North Dakota]]. they later married and had a child. They were imprisoned for theft and murder until Rorschach II freed them on Ozymandias' behalf. Ozymandias offers Marionette and Mime an opportunity to find their son if they help him find Doctor Manhattan.{{efn-ur|name=#1}} Arriving in the DC Universe via one of Nite Owl's old owlships, Mime and Marionette wander the streets of Gotham City, eventually entering Joker's territory.{{efn-ur|name=#2}} After killing Joker's henchmen in an altercation at a bar, they decide to find the villain.{{efn-ur|name=#3}} They finally meet Joker at the top of the Gotham City Police Department as one of Joker's henchmen brings the defeated body of Batman to him.{{efn-ur|name=#5}} The pair accompanies Joker to Riddler's underground villain meeting, which is crashed by the Comedian. The Comedian catches up to Mime and Marionette, but Joker uses his joy buzzer on the Comedian to save them. Joker admits that he has taken a liking to Mime and Marionette.{{efn-ur|name=#6}} On Joker's funhouse, Manhattan reveals why he spared them, besides they expect a second child. Mime and Marionette then escape with Comedian and the Lantern Battery.{{efn-ur|name=#7}} After Manhattan restored the DC Universe, he asks Mime and Marionette to stay, hoping to give them a second chance in raising their daughter Anita, and also because he already planned to reunite them with their other son Clark.{{efn-ur|name=#12}}
* '''Nostalgia / Cleopatra Pak''' — An orphan girl residing outside of Veidt's imprisonment who takes in Bubastis II.{{efn-ur|name=#12}}
* '''[[Rorschach (Reggie Long)|Rorschach II / Reggie Long]]''' — The son of Malcolm and Gloria Long, who becomes Walter Kovacs's successor as the new Rorschach. Ozymandias then recruits him as his new employee in his quest to find Doctor Manhattan where their search for him takes them to the DC Universe. When his parents died during the New York massacre, Reggie was traumatized by the event, and put into a psychiatric institution, where Mothman taught him how to fight and defend himself. Reginald then reads Kovacs' journal and learns that Adrian Veidt caused the massacre. After escaping the institution, Reggie became Rorschach II and initially planned to kill Ozymandias out of revenge. However, Reggie decided to spare Adrian after the latter claimed he had terminal cancer.{{efn-ur|name=#4}} Ozymandias then recruits Reginald as his new employee in his quest to find Doctor Manhattan; their search for him takes them to the DC universe.{{efn-ur|name=#1}} After meeting Batman, who locks him up on Arkham Asylum, Reggie is protected by Zebra-Man, and later rescued by Saturn Girl.{{efn-ur|name=#2}}{{efn-ur|name=#4}} The two of them escape Arkham to save John Thunder from a gang of thugs.{{efn-ur|name=#5}} When everyone is reunited in Joker's fun house, Batman reconciles with Reggie. When Doctor Manhattan appears and reveals Adrian was lying about his cancer, Reggie hits Adrian in retaliation for lying to him. He then takes off his mask and declares "Rorschach is dead" before leaving, with Batman trying to follow him, to no avail.{{efn-ur|name=#7}} Reggie was later seen holding a sign as the riots occur.{{efn-ur|name=#9}} After some persuasion from Batman and Alfred, Reggie becomes Rorschach again. He continues to operate as Rorschach when returned to his world.{{efn-ur|name=#12}}
* '''[[List of Watchmen characters#Alien Monster|The Squid]]''' — The "alien monster" summoned by Ozymandias that caused the New York massacre. Rorschach II had nightmares about it.{{efn-ur|name=#3}}

===Department of Metahuman Affairs===
The '''Department of Metahuman Affairs (DMA)''' is a government agency established by Martin Stein that created its own metahumans in the [[United States]]. Among those involved are:

* '''[[Martin Stein]]''' — Ronald Raymond's partner as Firestorm and former member of the Justice League, who was revealed by Doctor Manhattan to be the Director of the DMA and the one responsible behind the Supermen Theory. He sustained that he founded the agency to help other metahumans like his son who died because he couldn't control his metagene.{{efn-ur|name=#8}} After Doctor Manhattan undoes the experiment that erased the Justice Society of America and the Legion of Super-Heroes, Martin Stein was arrested for his involvement with the Department of Metahuman Affairs.{{efn-ur|name=#12}}
** '''[[Firestorm (comics)|Firestorm]] / [[Ronnie Raymond (comics)|Ronald Raymond]]''' — A superhero partnered with Martin Stein, unaware he was being used by the DMA. When Killer Frost claimed that Firestorm is a government agent, he denied any knowledge of it. His powers are later manipulated by Doctor Manhattan, turning Russian citizens into glass statues, with Bubastis II causing an explosion around him.{{efn-ur|name=#8}}{{efn-ur|name=#11}} When Ronald confronted Doctor Manhattan, the latter revealed that Stein orchestrated their transformation at purpose.{{efn-ur|name=#9}} Following Martin Stein's arrest, Ronald undergoes treatment at the All-Star Clinic which is Doctor Mid-Nite's medical clinic.{{efn-ur|name=#12}}
* '''[[Elasti-Girl|Elasti-Girl / Rita Farr]]''' — Former actress and former member of the Doom Patrol. She was later seen with the Doom Patrol when they go to confront Doctor Manhattan on Mars.{{efn-ur|name=#8}}
* '''[[Element Girl|Element Girl / Urania Blackwell]]''' — A former spy whose powers were unstable, causing her to commit suicide.
* '''[[Helga Jace]]''' — A Markovian scientist who exposes the Supermen Theory.{{efn-ur|name=#2}}
* '''[[Hyena (comics)|Hyena / Summer Day]]''' — Hyena was mentioned to have been the product of the Department of Metahuman affairs as a news report mentions her alongside Firestorm and Killer Frost.{{efn-ur|name=#12}}
* '''Moonbow / Brittany Brandon''' — An agent of the DMA who infiltrated inside the League of Villainy and was later confirmed to be associated with them.
* '''[[Typhoon (comics)|Typhoon / David Drake]]''' — An agent of the Department of Metahuman Affairs who infiltrated inside the League of Villainy before being shot and killed by the Comedian. Typhoon's involvement with the DMA was confirmed after his death as Stein gives orders to retrieve his body.
