'''Timothy Aloysius Cadwallader''' "'''Dum Dum'''" '''Dugan''' is a [[fictional character]] appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]]. He is an officer of [[S.H.I.E.L.D.]] and is one of the most experienced members of [[Nick Fury]]'s team, known for his marksmanship with [[rifle]]s and impressive physique. He is recognized for his trademark [[bowler hat]].

[[Neal McDonough]] appeared as the character in the 2011 [[Marvel Cinematic Universe]] film ''[[Captain America: The First Avenger]]'', the 2013 [[Marvel Studios]] short film ''[[Agent Carter (film)|Agent Carter]]'' (post-credits), in 2014 in the 1st episode of season 2 of ''[[Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D.]]'', and also in 2015 in the fifth episode of the first season of Marvel's ''[[Agent Carter (TV series)|Agent Carter]]''. McDonough returned as an alternate version of the character in the [[Disney+]] animated series ''[[What If...? (TV series)|What If...?]]'' (2021).

Fury and his Howling Commandos]]'' #1 (May 1963), and was created by [[Stan Lee]] and [[Jack Kirby]].<ref>{{cite book |last1=DeFalco |first1=Tom |last2=Sanderson |first2=Peter |last3=Brevoort |first3=Tom |last4=Teitelbaum |first4=Michael |last5=Wallace |first5=Daniel |last6=Darling |first6=Andrew |last7=Forbeck |first7=Matt |last8=Cowsill |first8=Alan |last9=Bray |first9=Adam |title=The Marvel Encyclopedia |date=2019 |publisher=DK Publishing |isbn=978-1-4654-7890-0 |page=119}}</ref>

==Fictional character biography==
Dum Dum Dugan was originally portrayed as a British citizen in ''Sgt Fury'' #1, but later [[Retroactive continuity|retconned]] to be born in [[Boston, Massachusetts]]. During [[World War II]], while working as a [[Strongman (circus)|circus strongman]], Dugan helps [[Nick Fury]] and [[Happy Sam Sawyer|Sam "Happy" Sawyer]] escape the Nazis during a mission, recounted in issue #34. Dugan joins the [[British Army]], and when Sawyer is charged with creating Fury's ''First Attack Squad'', formally listed as "Able Company" and nicknamed the ''[[Howling Commandos]]'', Sawyer invites Dugan to transfer into the [[US Army]] and become Fury's second-in-command. Dugan's exceptional strength saves the day in several of his adventures in the ''Sgt. Fury'' [[comic books]]. Dugan is an enlisted man with the rank of corporal, and wears the [[chevron (insignia)|chevrons]]  of his rank on the front top of his trademark bowler hat throughout World War II.{{issue|date=December 2017}}

Dugan leaves the U.S. Army before the [[Korean War]], but rejoins during the war as [[Second Lieutenant]] under the promoted [[First Lieutenant]] Fury, once again as his second-in-command of the reformed Howling Commandos.  Fury had already received a battlefield promotion to Second Lt. earlier.<ref>''Sgt. Fury and his Howling Commandos'' Annual #2. Marvel Comics.</ref>  Dugan remains with Fury when Fury continues his military career into the [[Vietnam War]], as shown in ''Sgt. Fury and his Howling Commandos'' Annual #3 and 4, and later into espionage work. Dugan's exact rank is not stated, but he is addressed as "Captain" at one point. He remains with Fury when Fury goes into the CIA and later into the original S.H.I.E.L.D.{{issue|date=December 2017}}

Different accounts state that Dugan and other members of Fury's Howling Commandos receive the Infinity Formula to explain how they all remain youthful and active despite being all into their 60s and 70s in the modern era. Other stories contradict this artificial maintenance of youth by Dugan as merely dyeing his hair and suffering a [[heart attack]].<ref>''Captain America'' #284</ref>

Later on in life, he is placed in charge of internal security on the S.H.I.E.L.D. [[Helicarrier]].{{issue|date=December 2017}}

Dum Dum is placed in charge of a SHIELD unit coined the [[Godzilla Squad]], charged with the task of tracking and stopping the radioactive [[kaiju]].<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Eury |first1=Michael |title=Flashback: Godzilla vs the Marvel Universe |journal=Back Issue |date=October 2019 |issue=#116 |page=57 |url=https://www.google.com/books/edition/Back_Issue_116/ABuzDwAAQBAJ?hl=en&gbpv=1&dq=%22dum+dum+dugan%22&pg=PA57&printsec=frontcover |access-date=4 April 2020}}</ref><ref>''Godzilla: King of the Monsters'' #1-24 (1977-79). Marvel Comics.</ref> Over the twenty-four issue series, Dugan goes from loathing the creature's destructive tendencies to accepting the beast's existence after [[Godzilla (comics)#Marvel Comics|Godzilla]] saves him on numerous occasions. Dugan later recalls a long list of his greatest nightmares, one of which includes Godzilla.{{Issue|date=April 2009}}

After suffering his heart attack, Dugan is formally promoted to Fury's permanent second-in-command of S.H.I.E.L.D. as "Deputy Director",<ref>''Captain America'' #284. Marvel Comics.</ref> though he has acted as such before his formal appointment.{{issue|date=December 2017}}

===Power struggles===
In ''Marvel Graphic Novel'' #18: ''She-Hulk'', Dum Dum Dugan ran S.H.I.E.L.D. during one of Nick Fury's absences. His authority is usurped by the craven, sex-obsessed agent [[Roger Dooley]]. [[She-Hulk]] and her boyfriend [[Wyatt Wingfoot]] are illegally captured. Dugan protests Dooley's forcible [[strip search]] of She-Hulk in public but is overruled. His authority is regained when Dooley is killed in action.{{issue|date=December 2017}}

===Nick Fury vs. S.H.I.E.L.D. (1988)===
During the ''Nick Fury vs. S.H.I.E.L.D.'' six issue series, Dum Dum Dugan and, later, the entirety of S.H.I.E.L.D., deal with another mutiny from within. Dugan was shown to have been shot to death while taking out the trash.<ref>''Nick Fury vs. S.H.I.E.L.D.'' #2. Marvel Comics.</ref> In this series, Hydra was revealed to have been a sister organization of S.H.I.E.L.D., with both directly controlled by the mysterious council, who had replaced many of the organization's top brass - including Deputy Director Dugan - with Life Model Decoy android duplicates in order to speed the survival of the fittest selection process that the council had been forcing between the two organizations over the decades.<ref>''Nick Fury vs. S.H.I.E.L.D.'' #5. Marvel Comics.</ref> All the officers who were thought to be killed were shown to have been replaced with Life Model Decoy android replacements and were found alive. Dugan retires, along with many of the older officers, at the end of this series.<ref>''Nick Fury vs. S.H.I.E.L.D.'' #6</ref> Dugan's retirement does not last long, as he rejoins Fury when the next version of S.H.I.E.L.D. (now known as Strategic Hazard Intervention Espionage Logistics Directorate) is created.<ref>''Nick Fury, Agent of S.H.I.E.L.D.'' vol. 2 #1 (1989). Marvel Comics.</ref>

Dugan calls on [[Squirrel Girl]] to help him take down various supervillains.<ref>{{Comic book reference | Writer = Dan Slott | Artist = Matt Haley | Story = Eggnog, Toilet Paper and Peace on Earth | Title = GLX-MAS Special | Date = December 2005 | Publisher = Marvel Comics}}</ref>

With Nick Fury's absence at the S.H.I.E.L.D. headquarters, Dugan is placed second-in-command to [[Maria Hill]].{{issue|date=November 2011}} Dugan is put in charge of mutant affairs, and deals with [[Wolverine (character)|Wolverine]].{{issue|date=November 2011}} Dugan apparently knows a dire secret to Logan's past.{{issue|date=November 2011}}

===Civil War===
In Marvel's ''[[Civil War (comics)|Civil War]]'' [[fictional crossover|crossover]], Dum Dum Dugan and several other S.H.I.E.L.D. agents are sent to capture his fugitive friend [[Captain America]]. The entire group is defeated.<ref>{{Comic book reference | Writer = Brian Michael Bendis | Penciller = Howard Chaykin | Inker = Jose Pimentel | Story = Disassembled | Title = The New Avengers | Issue = #21 | Date = Aug. 2006 | Publisher = Marvel Comics}}</ref> He expresses regret to fellow agent [[Sharon Carter]] for trying to capture his friend.<ref>{{Comic book reference | Writer = Ed Brubaker | Artist = Mike Perkins | Story = The Drums of War | Title = Captain America | Volume = 5 | Issue = #22 | Date = Nov. 2006 | Publisher = Marvel Comics}}</ref> He also states his concerns about losing faith in S.H.I.E.L.D due to the war and the way the organization is run under Director [[Maria Hill]].{{Issue|date=August 2011}}

After the events of ''Civil War'' and as Tony Stark takes over as director of S.H.I.E.L.D., Dugan turns in his letter of resignation, stating that he does not approve of the changes Stark has made, or the way he runs the organization. Dugan's resignation is not accepted however, as he is too vital to S.H.I.E.L.D.; despite his disapproval of Stark's leadership, Stark still trusts Dugan, and Dugan alone, with the command of a weapon that could send an abandoned Manhattan into the Negative Zone, destroying the island and anyone inside during ''[[World War Hulk]]'', stating that Hulk and the Warbound are too dangerous a global threat to leave Manhattan free. Stark trusts that Dugan will know the appropriate time to execute the plan, if at all. Dugan never used the weapon.{{Issue|date=August 2011}}

Dugan later proved his worth and loyalty to S.H.I.E.L.D during a confrontation with Maria Hill, where he forced her to confront the fact that she was apparently willing to take actions that would allow innocent people to die while still sticking to 'the book' because the alternative was to disobey orders, informing her that some situations weren't in 'the book'.<ref>''Iron Man'' #26</ref> Inspired by Dugan's words, Hill ended up putting her career on the line by locking down the [[United Nations]] under S.H.I.E.L.D. martial law so Stark could escape a tribunal and track down the [[Mandarin (comics)|Mandarin]], with Dugan providing Stark with a version of the [[Iron Man's armor#Silver Centurion|Silver Centurion]] armor after his Extremis abilities were deactivated.<ref>''Iron Man'' #28. Marvel Comics.</ref>

Dum Dum is stabbed through the chest by Wolverine's son [[Daken]].<ref>''Wolverine Origins'' #10. Marvel Comics.</ref>

===Secret Invasion===
It was later revealed that, shortly after Captain America's death, Dugan was ambushed and stabbed by a [[Skrull]] impersonating [[Valentina Allegra de la Fontaine|Contessa Valentina Allegra de la Fontaine]] who then took his place.<ref>''Secret Invasion Prologue''. Marvel Comics.</ref> The Dugan impostor destroys the Peak, [[S.W.O.R.D. (comics)|S.W.O.R.D.]]'s Orbital base, in a suicide strike that allows the Skrull armada to invade Earth.<ref>''Secret Invasion'' #1. Marvel Comics.</ref> After the invasion, [[Iron Man]] discovers the Skrull kidnap victims alive and well, including Dum Dum.<ref>''Secret Invasion'' #8. Marvel Comics.</ref> Dugan is later shown in a support group meeting with the others that had been replaced by Skrulls. Dugan wanted to leave immediately but was convinced to stay.<ref>''Avengers: The Initiative'' #20. Marvel Comics.</ref>

===Secret Warriors===
After a confrontation that goes badly between Nick Fury's [[Secret Warriors (Team White)|Secret Warriors]] and [[Gorgon (Tomi Shishido)|The Gorgon]], Fury calls up Dum Dum Dugan's "Howling Commandos" [[private military company|PMC]] for help in Fury's missions, needing "a couple of nasty old bastards with a bad habit of shooting first and asking questions later." Dugan learns that the new S.H.I.E.L.D. organization has been controlled by their old enemies HYDRA right from the start,<ref>''Secret Warriors'' #1-3 (2010). Marvel Comics.</ref> the same as the older incarnations of S.H.I.E.L.D.<ref>''Nick Fury vs. SHIELD'' #4 (1991). Marvel Comics.</ref> Dugan later shows up in Fury's efforts to bring down HYDRA and yet another secretive organization, the Russian [[Leviathan (Marvel Comics)|Leviathan]].<ref>''Secret Warriors'' #10-11 (2011). Marvel Comics.</ref> Dugan and Jasper Sitwell soon became all that is left of the Howling Commandos PMC after battles with HYDRA and Leviathan and are arrested by the U.N.. At the end of ''[[Secret Warriors (2009 series)|Secret Warriors]]'', Dugan and Sitwell were released.{{Issue|date=August 2011}}

Dugan is recruited as part of a multi-verse wide effort to stop a supernatural-powered Nazi zombie army. Assisted by [[Howard the Duck]] he takes a team of warriors to the affected reality and manages to neutralize the threat at the source.<ref>''Marvel Zombies Destroy!'' #1-5 (2012). Marvel Comics.</ref>

A HYDRA group attempts to devastate civilization via super-powered shock-troops and media that inflames people's personal beliefs into irrationality. Dugan leads the effort in destroying this group, often with high explosives. He works closely with Sharon Carter and the [[Falcon (comics)|Falcon]]. Dugan is severely wounded in the upper arm but recovers.<ref>''Captain America'' vol. 7 #15-19 (2013). Marvel Comics.</ref>

===Original Sin===
During the ''[[Original Sin (comics)|Original Sin]]'' storyline, it is revealed Dum Dum Dugan was killed in 1966 while on a Black Ops mission, and Nick Fury - the only other person who knew about the mission and Dugan's death - had his body preserved and Dugan's mind tied into a transmission device that projected his consciousness into an advanced [[Life Model Decoy|LMD]]; Fury claimed that this was done because he did not want to lose his best friend and felt that he needed Dugan to serve as his conscience. When Dugan finds out, he accuses Fury of doing this so that he could feel guilty about things, reasoning that Fury could convince himself that he was still a hero if he felt bad about his actions as the man on the wall. Telling Fury not to bring him back again if they were ever friends, Dugan thinking he was nothing but a mechanical fraud and Fury saying nothing to let Dugan think otherwise, then shoots himself in the head.<ref>''Original Sins'' #5. Marvel Comics.</ref>

===Involvement with S.T.A.K.E.===
Months later, Maria Hill deemed it necessary to bring the LMD of Dugan back and removed the blocks that prevented his resurrection so he could return to Area 13 and help S.T.A.K.E. (short for the Special Threat Assessment for Known Extranormalities).<ref>''S.H.I.E.L.D.'' vol. 3 #9. Marvel Comics.</ref>

Dum Dum Dugan is later appointed to lead the latest incarnation of the Howling Commandos.<ref name="ReferenceA">''Howling Commandos of S.H.I.E.L.D.'' #6. Marvel Comics.</ref>

During the ''[[Avengers: Standoff!]]'' storyline, Dum Dum Dugan discovered that Dr. Paul Kraye had [[Maria Hill]] imprison [[Orrgo]] at Pleasant Hill. Upon locating Pleasant Hill, Dum Dum Dugan leads the Howling Commandos to Pleasant Hill where they fight past the inmates. When they confront Kobik, she teleports the Howling Commandos back to S.T.A.K.E. HQ. Once back at S.T.A.K.E. HQ, Dum Dum Dugan learns from Orrgo that Paul Kraye released all the inmates there to cause havoc, causing the Howling Commandos to spring into action.<ref name="ReferenceA"/>

===Involvement with C.R.A.D.L.E.===
During the "[[Outlawed (comics)|Outlawed]]" storyline, Dum Dum Dugan appears as a member of C.R.A.D.L.E. when a law is passed that forbids superheroes who are below the age of 21.<ref>''Outlawed'' #1. Marvel Comics.</ref> Dum Dum Dugan led some C.R.A.D.L.E. agents to arrest [[Miles Morales|Spider-Man]] for teen vigilantism. But Spider-Man escapes them, leaving Dugan embarrassed for being defeated. C.R.A.D.L.E. uses Spider-Man's prior history and came too close to him at Brooklyn Visions Academy.<ref>''Miles Morales: Spider-Man'' #17. Marvel Comics.</ref>

===1602===
In ''[[Marvel 1602]]'', the commander of Sir Nicholas Fury's soldiers is named Dougan.<ref>''Marvel 1602'' (2003). Marvel Comics.</ref> In ''[[1602: New World]]'', he is the defender of the [[Roanoke Colony]].<ref>''1602: New World'' (2005). Marvel Comics.</ref>

===Earth X===
Dugan appears, on the other side of the afterlife, in the climactic battle against Mephisto's forces in ''[[Earth X]]'' Volume 1, issue 'X'. Along with multiple modern superheroes, Dugan fights with many of his old 'Howling Commandos' comrades. He had perished due to being taken by the Hydra entity.<ref>''Universe X: Cap'' 2000. Marvel Comics.</ref>

===The Transformers===
Dum Dum Dugan and [[Nick Fury]] show up in the issue "Prisoner of War!" from ''[[The Transformers (Marvel Comics)|The Transformers]]'' as guest characters along with [[Spider-Man|Peter Parker]] and [[Robbie Robertson (comics)|Joe Robertson]]. They also reference Godzilla's comic in this appearance, though not by name, due to Marvel no longer holding the licence at the time.{{issue|date=December 2017}}

===Ultimate Marvel===
Dugan appears in ''[[Ultimate X-Men]]'' several times, once at the end of the ''Blockbuster'' storyline,<ref name="Ultimate X-Men #39">''Ultimate X-Men'' #39. Marvel Comics.</ref> then again, during ''New Mutants'',<ref name="Ultimate X-Men #39"/> and one more time as a hologram at the beginning of the ''Magnetic North'' story arc. Here, Dugan is scarred and aging but physically fit, and is S.H.I.E.L.D.'s Director of Mutant Operations. He has a mysterious connection to Wolverine, even remembering Logan's real name while Wolverine himself couldn't recall it.<ref name="Ultimate X-Men #39"/> Also of note is that this version of Dugan has never been seen with the mainstream version's trademark derby hat. In the "[[Ultimate Origins]]" five-parter it is shown that he was involved with [[Weapon Plus|Project: Rebirth]], the project that made Captain America, appearing as an already grown man in 1943, essentially replacing [[Chester Phillips]] in Mainstream continuity.<ref>''Ultimate Origins'' #1 (June 2008). Marvel Comics.</ref> Thanks to [[Ultimate Wolverine|a runaway mutant]], Dugan and [[Ultimate Nick Fury|Nick Fury]] led a strike team at the [[Weapon X|Weapon X facility]], but not before killing [[Malcolm Colcord]] on the true origins behind mutants, and rescuing [[Black Panther (comics)|T'Challa Udaku]].<ref>''Ultimate Origins'' #5. Marvel Comics.</ref>
