'''Elysius''' is a fictional character appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]].

Elysius is an artificially created being constructed by the Titanian super computer [[ISAAC (comics)|ISAAC]], and possesses some of the superhuman attributes of an [[Eternal (comics)|Eternal]] from [[Titan (Marvel Comics location)|Titan]].  She became the lover of [[Mar-Vell]], and after his death used his genetic material to impregnate herself with [[Genis-Vell]] and [[Phyla-Vell]].

==Fictional character biography==
Elysius was created by [[ISAAC (comics)|ISAAC]], the sentient computer system of [[Titan (Marvel Comics location)|Titan]]. She serves as ISAAC's lieutenant, and aided ISAAC in its conquest of Titan.<ref>''Captain Marvel'' #59-60</ref>

She later aided [[Captain Mar-Vell]], and [[Drax the Destroyer]] against [[ISAAC (comics)|ISAAC]], [[Stellarax]], Lord Gaea, and Chaos. She then began her romance with Mar-Vell.<ref>''Captain Marvel'' #61-62; ''Marvel Spotlight'' vol. 2 #1-2</ref> She returned to Earth with Mar-Vell and [[Rick Jones (comics)|Rick Jones]].<ref>''Marvel Spotlight'' vol. 2 #3</ref>

She later accompanied Mar-Vell to Denver to visit Rick Jones.<ref>''The Incredible Hulk'' vol. 2 #245</ref> She accompanied [[Betty Ross Banner|Betty Ross]], Rick Jones, and Fred Sloan to Gamma Base in search of Mar-Vell and the [[Hulk]]. She departed Gamma Base with Mar-Vell.<ref>''The Incredible Hulk'' vol. 2 #247-248</ref>

Some time later, Elysius attended the death-watch of Captain Mar-Vell.<ref>''Marvel Graphic Novel'' #1</ref>

Later, she remained on Titan when [[Starfox (comics)|Starfox]] departed for Earth to join the [[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]].<ref>''The Avengers'' #230</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
Elysius is an artificially created being patterned on and possessing some of the attributes of the Titanians, a lesser sub-branch of the long-lived offshoot of humanity called the [[Eternal (comics)|Eternals]]. She has enhanced stamina, and her strength, speed, and durability are higher than that of a human, although she does not possess the ability to fly or levitate.  She has limited telepathic abilities of an unrevealed nature. She keeps griffin-like creatures which obey her telepathic commands.

She uses an alien hand-weapon capable of disrupting force fields or of firing energy of an unspecified nature as bursts of concussive force. [[ISAAC (comics)|ISAAC]] crafted her personal sky-ship, which resembles an ancient Earth galleon but possesses the capacity of flight.
