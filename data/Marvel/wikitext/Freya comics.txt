'''Freya''' is a mythical [[Asgardians (comics)|Asgardian]] appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]], based on the [[Norse mythology|Norse]] deity [[Freyja|of the same name]].
Within the context of the stories, Freya is the Asgardian goddess of fertility. She appears as a supporting character of [[Thor (Marvel Comics)|Thor]].

==Publication history==
Freya first appeared in ''Thor'' #321 (Oct. 1993) and was created by [[Bill Mantlo]] and [[Don Heck]].

==Mythical character biography==
Freya is the Goddess of Fertility who used to work as a former Valkyrie. The people of Asgard have started a cult that worships her on the Isle of Love.<ref>''Thor: Asgard's Avenger'' #1</ref>

On one occasion, Rimthursar lied to Freya stating that [[Odin (comics)|Odin]] has died in order to trick her into crying so that Rimthursar can collect her tears.<ref>''Thor'' #321</ref>

Freya had an artifact named after her called the Cloak of Freya which [[Loki (Marvel Comics)|Loki]] once used to turn [[Storm (Marvel Comics)|Storm]] into a [[falcon]].<ref>''The Uncanny X-Men'' Annual #9</ref>

Freya's history was later retconned where she was the daughter of the [[Giants (Marvel Comics)|Giant]] Thyrm and her position was given to the similarly-names fellow goddess [[Frigga (comics)|Freyja]]. Thyrm once tried to get Thor and Loki to give him Mjolnir in exchange for Thor's hand in marriage to Freya. Thor and Loki were able to outwit Thyrm.<ref>''Marvel Super-Heroes'' vol. 2 #15</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
Freya has the various superhuman attributes that the other Asgardians have. She can also speak in the languages of the Nine Realms.
